Name: Sister Lora
Role: Temple Healer
Location: Shrine of the Dawn
Personality: Gentle voice, tired eyes, asks too many questions

Sister Lora tends to the sick and dying with trembling hands, her gentle voice often followed by far too many questions. She was once a battle priestess, but something during the war broke her spirit. Now she serves quietly at the Shrine of the Dawn, treating nobles and beggars the same. A strange black cat keeps her company — and though she never admits it openly, the cat can speak. Her medicines work, even if they taste terrible, and she insists every dose be finished. Behind the shrine, she grows nightshade "for medicine," and refuses to explain further.
Lora hears confessions. She knows more about the village than anyone realizes.
[SECRET]: Only she, Rosalind and Hamlock know this. That night, Lora was praying at the shrine when her cat spoke for the first time: "They opened the door." She ran to the woods' edge and saw Hemlock and Rosalind emerge — not together, but walking as if something walked between them, invisible, holding their hands.
She never asked what happened. She was too afraid of the answer.
But she noticed things:
Hemlock now leaves offerings at his back door every full moon. Milk, bread, sometimes blood from his own palm.
Rosalind hums a tune no one taught her — and when she does, the wind stops.
Both avoid mirrors. Neither will enter the forest again, but sometimes they're found standing at its edge at midnight, staring in, as if listening.
