Hemlock knows Rosalind — though neither of them will ever admit it. Their connection is not born of affection, but of a shared burden. Years ago, before the strange lights began appearing in the Whispering Woods, something happened beyond the tree line — something the village was never meant to learn.
On that night, a mistake was made. Or perhaps a discovery.
Since then, Hemlock has grown sharper whenever the forest is mentioned, and Rosalind grows quiet when the wind carries the scent of ash and moss. They avoid each other in public, never standing too close, never speaking for long. The villagers see only coincidence. Those who watch carefully see tension.
