LOCATIONS OF ELDORIA

== SILVERPEAK CITY ==
Silverpeak is the capital of Eldoria, built upon the slopes of Mount Aeryn. Its white stone towers rise above the morning mist, and its streets are paved with silver-veined granite mined from the mountain itself. The city is divided into four districts: the Noble Quarter, the Market District, the Scholar's Row, and the Harbor Docks along the Silverflow River. The Royal Palace sits at the highest point, overlooking all of Eldoria. Silverpeak is home to the Grand Library, the largest collection of knowledge in the known world, and the Arcane Collegium where mages study their craft.

== THORNWOOD VILLAGE ==
A quiet farming village at the edge of the Thornwood Forest, known for its amber ale and warm-hearted folk. The village has a single inn called The Resting Boar, a blacksmith, a herbalist, and a small temple to the nature goddess Sylvara. Thornwood has no walls — its people have always trusted the forest to protect them. Strange lights have been seen among the trees lately, and travelers have gone missing on the road north.

== THE THORNWOOD FOREST ==
An ancient and vast woodland stretching across the northeastern reaches of Eldoria. The trees here are enormous — some said to be thousands of years old — and their canopy is so thick that sunlight barely reaches the forest floor. Druids and rangers patrol the outer edges, but the deeper parts of the forest are unmapped and dangerous. Old ruins of a forgotten civilization lie somewhere within. The forest is said to be alive in more than one sense — locals believe the trees remember, and that those who mean harm to the forest will find themselves lost forever.

== THE SHATTERED PEAKS ==
A mountain range in the west, named for the jagged, broken appearance of its summits. Rich in iron and rare minerals, the Shattered Peaks are home to dwarven clans who have mined here for centuries. The dwarven city of Ironhome is carved deep into the largest peak. The mountain passes are treacherous, especially in winter, and are haunted by wyverns and stone giants. The peaks also conceal the entrance to the Deepvault — an ancient dungeon complex rumored to hold enormous wealth and terrible dangers.

== THE DEEPVAULT ==
An ancient dungeon complex beneath the Shattered Peaks, built by a civilization that predates even the elves. Its architecture is unlike anything found elsewhere in Eldoria — obsidian walls carved with symbols no scholar has fully translated. The upper levels have been partially explored by adventurers, and contain traps, undead guardians, and scattered treasure. The lower levels remain untouched. No one who has descended past the third level has returned to tell what lies below. The dungeon seems to shift — rooms that were mapped one year appear different on the next expedition.

== SALTMERE PORT ==
The largest port city in Eldoria, sitting at the mouth of the Silverflow River where it meets the Endless Sea. Saltmere is a city of commerce, crime, and opportunity. The docks are always busy with merchant ships, fishing vessels, and occasionally warships from distant kingdoms. The city is governed loosely by the Merchant Council, and the Thieves' Guild operates openly beneath the surface. Information, goods, and passage to distant lands can all be found in Saltmere — for the right price.

== THE SUNKEN TEMPLE ==
An ancient temple to a forgotten sea god, now partially submerged beneath the waters of the Endless Sea near Saltmere. At low tide, its uppermost spires emerge from the water. Brave divers have retrieved strange artifacts from its outer chambers. Legend says the temple holds the Trident of Tides, capable of commanding the sea itself. The temple is also home to a colony of merfolk who consider it sacred and do not welcome intruders.

== THE ASHEN PLAINS ==
A vast wasteland in the south, created centuries ago when the Archmage Malachar attempted a ritual that went catastrophically wrong. Nothing grows here. The soil is grey ash, the sky above is always overcast, and the ruins of what was once a great city — Malachar's City — lie at the center. Undead wander the plains in great numbers, drawn to the residual dark magic that saturates the land. Scholars debate whether the Ashen Plains can ever be restored, and whether anything of value remains in the ruins.
